Methods

In the original Diffusion Autoencoder, the encoder produces a non-spatial latent vector capturing the semantic properties of the image. In contrast, the encoder of our Frequency-Masked Diffusion Autoencoder produces a latent feature map whose spatial dimensions equal that of the image. We train a diffusion model (the "decoder"), conditioned on a frequency-masked version of this feature map.

Figure 1 shows a diagram of our Frequency-Masked Diffusion Autoencoder, which consists of three main components.

Training

Each training iteration consists of three steps:
  • First, the encoder converts an image into a latent feature map with the same spatial resolution.
  • Second, we randomly mask this feature map in the frequency domain. We provide more details for this in the next section.
  • Third, the decoder attempts to reconstruct the input image from the masked feature map, and a noisy version of the image.

In other words, the decoder is a conditional diffusion model, where the conditions are masked feature maps. We train both the encoder and the diffusion model (decoder) simulatenously.
